Problem Statement:
The current carbon credit ecosystem is inefficient, opaque, and prone to misuse, especially in the way carbon credits are traded. Companies often rely on static emission limits and outdated reporting systems, allowing them to repeatedly purchase credits instead of actively reducing their emissions. This turns carbon trading into a loophole rather than a solution, enabling high-polluting organizations to continue unsustainable practices without accountability. Additionally, the lack of real-time monitoring, intelligent verification, and transparent transaction systems increases the risk of data manipulation, greenwashing, and unfair credit exchange. Existing platforms also fail to incorporate dynamic pricing, predictive analysis, and supply chain emissions, making the system reactive and ineffective. As a result, the carbon market does not successfully incentivize genuine emission reduction or build trust among stakeholders.
